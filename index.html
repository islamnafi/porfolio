About

Hi, I’m Nafiul Islam. My journey has been anything but conventional, blending engineering, computer science, and business into one exciting narrative.
I earned my bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering from the Military Institute of Science and Technology, where I explored a wide array of subjects. While mechanical design, robotics, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and heat transfer intrigued me, my true passion always lay elsewhere: computers. My fascination with technology began in 8th grade when I was introduced to programming and the magic of what happens behind the scenes. That first spark turned into a lifelong interest in both hardware and software. I even spent my free time building computers, because why stop at knowing how they work when you can assemble them yourself?
The competitive nature of university admissions in Bangladesh led me to mechanical engineering instead of my first choice, computer science. While it wasn’t my initial passion, I embraced the challenge, and along the way, I discovered a strong interest in composite materials, manufacturing, and production processes. These experiences broadened my perspective and shaped my analytical and problem-solving skills.
Determined to pursue my passion, I transitioned into computer science after graduation by enrolling in a master’s program at United International University. Yes, it was an unconventional shift, but I see it as an opportunity to combine my engineering background with the computational skills I’ve always wanted to develop.
Alongside academics, I’ve also ventured into business development, focusing on the IT and energy sectors. This career path has given me valuable insight into bridging technical expertise with strategic thinking. On top of that, I dedicate time to personal projects, combining creativity with technical problem-solving to keep learning and growing.
Through it all, I’ve learned that whether it’s engineering, programming, or business, passion and persistence can create the most rewarding outcomes—even if the path isn’t the one you originally planned.
